Jane Goodall

British ethologist

Jane Goodall with the chimpanzee she observed

Jane Goodall, in full Dame Jane Goodall, original name Valerie Jane Morris-Goodall, British ethologist, known for her exceptionally detailed and long-term research on the chimpanzees of Gombe Stream National Park in Tanzania.

Here's a time line of Jane Goodall's life:

  • 1934 -Born in London England

  • 1952 -left school,worked as a secretary and as a film production assistant until she gained passage to Africa.

  • 1960 -began assisting paleontologist and anthropologist Louis Leakey，established a camp in the Gombe Stream Game Reserve (now a national park) so that she could observe the behaviour of chimpanzees in the region.

  • 1964 - married a Dutch photographer Baron Hugo van Lawick who had been sent in 1962 to Tanzania to film her work

  • 1965 -The University of Cambridge awarded Goodall a Ph.D. in ethology

  • 1967 -the couple had a son and later divorced

  • 1977 -cofounded the Jane Goodall Institute for Wildlife Research, Education and Conservation (commonly called the Jane Goodall Institute) in California; the centre later moved its headquarters to the Washington, D.C., area

  • 1986 -She summarized her years of observation in The Chimpanzees of Gombe: Patterns of Behavior

  • 1991 -created Jane Goodall’s Roots & Shoots (1991), a youth service program.

  • 2002 -she became a UN Messenger of Peace.

  • 2003 -Goodall was created Dame Commander of the Order of the British Empire (DBE)

  • 2017 -Jane, a documentary about her life and work, appeared

  • 2021 -was awarded the 2021 Templeton Prize

  • 2022 -was awarded the 2022 Stephen Hawking Medal for Science Communication
